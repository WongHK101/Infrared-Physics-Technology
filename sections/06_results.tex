\section{Results}

\subsection{Pairwise Baseline Comparison}

inference setting, and XoFTR-640 reaches 494/500. This result sharpens the role of UAV-TAlign. The
framework targets the decision layer that remains after strong pairwise matching is available.

\subsection{Scene-Level Performance of UAV-TAlign}

High pairwise usability does not automatically imply broad scene retention, because the canonical
decision answers a different operating question. UAV-TAlign retains 7 of 15 scenes with a mean
accepted-frame ratio of 64.5\%. This operating point emphasizes high-confidence transforms and
shows how robust consensus and verification turn abundant frame-level matches into selected
alignment products.

\subsection{Protocol and Proxy Evidence}

QA-filtered decisions are more strongly associated with QA-relative outlier signals than with
simple matcher availability. The baseline-relative QA outlier signal separates all 8 filtered
scenes from the 7 retained scenes. Accepted-frame ratio is also informative as part of the joint
decision: scene 02 has 98\% accepted frames but is filtered because reject and QA outlier signals
remain high, whereas scene 04 is retained with 80\% accepted frames when these signals are absent.
These observations show that UAV-TAlign captures transform usability as a joint QA decision rather
than a simple count of matched frames.

\centering

\subsection{Cumulative Ablation}

The strongest module-level gain is produced by robust scene consensus, while deterministic
selection is best understood as a reproducible operating policy. Adding robust scene consensus
substantially improves edge and gradient alignment proxies while reducing severe outliers. Adding
QA-aware verification then converts accepted-frame evidence into a reliability-controlled
scene-level criterion, matching the formal 8-scene slice at 5/8 retained scenes.
