\title{UAV-TAlign-12K: A Real-World Cross-FOV UAV Infrared-Visible\\ Dataset and Scene-Level Reliability Benchmark}

\author[anon]{Anonymous Authors}
\affiliation[anon]{organization={Anonymous submission}}

\begin{abstract}
Reliable UAV infrared-visible alignment is important for aerial inspection, nighttime monitoring,
and multimodal thermal sensing, yet existing resources provide limited support for evaluating
real captured cross-FOV alignment at the scene level. We introduce UAV-TAlign-12K, which contains
6,039 real-world UAV infrared-visible pairs across 15 scenes and provides an integrity-checked
6,037-pair official evaluation split. The dataset spans day, night, and low-light
capture, grayscale and pseudocolor thermal rendering, and wide, zoom, and standard UAV views. We
further define a manifest-fixed, label-free benchmark that separates pairwise homography evidence
from scene-level retained alignment and reports condition-aware and risk--coverage diagnostics.
Evaluation with six off-the-shelf baselines shows that pairwise evidence is broadly available:
raw MINIMA and RoMa produce homographies for all 6,037 evaluated pairs, while LoFTR and XoFTR
remain near-saturated. As a scene-level reference method, UAV-TAlign aggregates frame evidence
through reliability-guided selection, robust consensus, and QA-aware verification. It produces
scene homographies for all 15 scenes and retains 9 under the strict canonical gate. Together, the
dataset, protocol, and reference method establish a reproducible benchmark for studying when
cross-FOV UAV infrared-visible alignment is reliable enough to retain.
\end{abstract}

\begin{highlights}
\item UAV-TAlign-12K provides 6,039 real cross-FOV UAV infrared-visible pairs.
\item The dataset spans diverse illumination, thermal rendering, and UAV view settings.
\item A manifest-fixed benchmark separates pairwise evidence from scene-level retention.
\item UAV-TAlign is a reliability-controlled scene-level reference method.
\end{highlights}
