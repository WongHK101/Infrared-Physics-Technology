\title{UAV-TAlign: Scene-Level RGB-Thermal Alignment for Cross-FOV UAV Imagery}

\author[anon]{Anonymous Authors}
\affiliation[anon]{organization={Anonymous submission}}

\begin{abstract}
UAV RGB-thermal alignment is a prerequisite for reliable aerial inspection, monitoring, and
nighttime multimodal perception. In real cross-FOV aerial imagery, the operating question is not
only whether two frames can be matched, but whether the resulting alignment can be trusted at the
scene level. Modern multimodal matchers can already produce abundant pairwise correspondences, but
viewpoint variation, cross-resolution sensing, thermal rendering changes, and low-light imagery
make the resulting frame-level homographies uneven in quality. This highlights a critical gap
between pairwise match availability and scene-level alignment reliability. We present
UAV-TAlign, a scene-level RGB-thermal alignment framework that turns strong pairwise multimodal
matching into reliable scene-level decisions through reliability-guided evidence selection, robust
scene consensus, and QA-aware verification. We further package UAV-TAlign-1K, a public
evaluation subset with a scalable label-free protocol for real UAV RGB-thermal alignment.
Experiments against classical and modern off-the-shelf baselines, protocol-consistency analysis,
and cumulative module ablations show that, once pairwise matching is largely available, the
strongest gains come from robust scene consensus and QA-aware scene retention.
\end{abstract}

\begin{highlights}
\item A scene-level RGB-thermal alignment framework is introduced for cross-FOV UAV imagery.
\item Robust scene consensus and QA-aware verification convert pairwise evidence into retained transforms.
\item UAV-TAlign-1K provides a focused public evaluation subset and scalable label-free protocol.
\item Baselines and ablations show that scene-level reliability remains critical after pairwise saturation.
\end{highlights}
