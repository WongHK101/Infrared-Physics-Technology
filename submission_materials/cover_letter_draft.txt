Dear Editor,

We submit our manuscript, "UAV-TAlign-12K: Scene-Level Reliability Evaluation for Cross-FOV UAV Infrared-Visible Image Alignment", for consideration as a research article in Infrared Physics & Technology.

This work addresses infrared-visible alignment for real UAV sensing, where cross-FOV capture, cross-resolution imaging, illumination variation, and thermal rendering changes make reliable scene-level registration a practical operating challenge. Rather than treating the problem only as pairwise image matching, the manuscript introduces UAV-TAlign-12K, a 6,039-pair UAV infrared-visible alignment collection with a 6,037-pair integrity-checked evaluation split, and a label-free scene-level reliability protocol for evaluating retained alignment products.

The manuscript further presents UAV-TAlign, a reliability-aware scene-level alignment framework that converts strong pairwise multimodal evidence into retained scene transforms through reliability-guided evidence selection, robust scene consensus, and QA-aware verification. Journal-scale experiments compare an infrared-visible classical baseline, RIFT2, with modern dense and learned matchers, and show that pairwise homography evidence is broadly available on UAV-TAlign-12K. The remaining operating question is scene-level reliability, which is analyzed through strict canonical scene retention and risk-coverage profiles.

We believe the manuscript fits Infrared Physics & Technology because it focuses on infrared-visible UAV imaging, practical thermal-visible registration, label-free reliability evaluation, and benchmark-scale experimental validation for deployable infrared sensing systems.

The dataset release materials, official evaluation manifest, metadata, integrity report, and evaluation scripts have been prepared for public release. The final repository, DOI, license, and reviewer-access link will be inserted after the corresponding author confirms the release route.

The manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. All authors have approved the submission.
